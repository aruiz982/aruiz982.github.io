El mapa del gasto en calefacción, edificio a edificio: ¿pagas más que tu vecino?

The heating expenditure map: Do you pay more than your neighbour?

The map shows the heating costs of Spanish homes, how much the same thing would cost if the building was more energy-efficient and how much the renovation would be. The article pretends to aim Spaniards to invest in their homes so they would not need as much heating power as they do, which would benefit their finances and the environment.

The data to create the map was extracted from Catastro and had to be reduced, so Mapbox could process it. For this, I used geopandas, a Python library for geospatial analysis and, because some files where really heavy, the command line with some libraries, like jq. After this, I uploaded the map to the server with Mapbox CLI and later styled it with Mapbox GL JS.

It was quite a challenge, considering the files we were working with were 12Gb heavy and that we had never made a project of this characteristics.

¿Dónde trabajan tus vecinos? El mapa de los trayectos obligados en cada municipio

Where do you neighbours work? The map of all mandatory journeys

The Spanish Ministry of Transportation had published very detailed information of how many people had to mandatorily move and where they had to go each day (they considered people that had to go to work, to university or school). We wanted to do something with the information and ended up creating this map with the most frecuent journeys that Spaniards made each day.

For this piece, we extracted the data and cartography from the ministry and analysed it geopandas in Python. We needed to merge both the csv and the geojson in order to get a way of painting all features. We extracted the centroids and later connected all lines with the information we had.

Later, we created the map with Mapbox. It included all journeys that Spaniards did with frequency of three weeks a month or more, the distances they had to cover and the most frequent destinations, considering every town available.

¿Tus vecinos están de alquiler o han comprado? Así es la vivienda en cada barrio

Do your neighbours rent or own their homes? This is the housing situation in each neighbourhood

This map was a complement to a housing piece to inform of the new census data regarding this a more topics. The map offers four different views: one for second homes, one for rental homes, one for one-person households and one for more than people households.

The piece was relatively easy to do and the only obstacle we found was the publishing date: we had to publish the article not long after the Spanish Statistics Institute released the information.

The data was analysed with the library pandas in Python and later joined to the cartography we had in Mapbox. We later created the map with the same tool.

España a remojo: el mapa de todas las piscinas, calle a calle

Spain soaking wet: the map of all pools, street by street

This was a very summer-like article that we published last year about how all swimming pools in Spain are distributed, where the bigger ones are located and also to show the lack of public pools in Spain.

This time, we also worked with information from Catastro, that has separate files for only this type of construction. We wanted to make something easy and fun, but we could not.

The map was once again created with Mapbox. This time, we could not paint the silhouettes and decided to get the centroids with QGIS and display those instead. All close-ups are created with QGIS that were later edited with Figma.
