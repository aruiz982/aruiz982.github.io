The heating expenditure map: Do you pay more than your neighbour?

El mapa del gasto en calefacción, edificio a edificio: ¿pagas más que tu vecino?

The map shows the heating costs of Spanish homes, how much the same thing would cost if the building was more energy-efficient and how much the renovation would be. The article pretends to aim Spaniards to invest in their homes so they would not need as much heating power as they do, which would benefit their finances and the environment.

Link

Python and Mapshaper in the command line to filter the information and Mapbox to create the map.

Where do you neighbours work? The map of all mandatory journeys

¿Dónde trabajan tus vecinos? El mapa de los trayectos obligados en cada municipio

The Spanish Ministry of Transportation had published very detailed information of how many people had to mandatorily move and where they had to go each day (they considered people that had to go to work, to university or school). We wanted to do something with the information and ended up creating this map with the most frecuent journeys that Spaniards made each day.

Link

Python to manipulate the information and to create the lines from the centroids given. Mapbox to create the map.

The map of all European population: how many people live in your square kilometre?

El mapa de toda la población europea: ¿cuánta gente vive en tu kilómetro cuadrado?

For this map we used a population grid provided by Eurostat. Our main goal was to show in a different form how population is distributed in Europe and how it has changed in the last ten years.

Link

Python to manipulate the data and join all geojsons involved. The command line to transform the file to .mbtiles. Mapbox to create the map.

Spain soaking wet: the map of all pools, street by street

España a remojo: el mapa de todas las piscinas, calle a calle

This was a very summer-like article that we published last year about how all swimming pools in Spain are distributed, where the bigger ones are located and also to show the lack of public pools in Spain. This time, we also worked with information from Catastro, that has separate files for only this type of construction. We wanted to make something easy and fun, but in the end we did not.

Link

Python to scrape the website from which the data was extracted and to reduce its size. Mapbox to create the map.
